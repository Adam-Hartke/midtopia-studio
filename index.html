Inside Midtopia Studio

About Midtopia Studio

Midtopia Studio is a premier recording and media production facility in Wichita, Kansas, rooted in the city's musical legacy and built for its creative future. The space began its life as Brickhouse Studios before transitioning into Magnitude Creative, where it became a vital hub for Wichita's music community. Following the untimely passing of beloved local music pioneer Kenny Ballinger, the studio evolved once again—this time becoming a dedicated creative home for Midtopia roster artists and a cornerstone in the region's independent music ecosystem.

Now under the stewardship of its new ownership team—Angelo Rodriguez and Jessie & Adam Hartke—Midtopia Studio has been revitalized and relaunched as a full-service, world-class recording and media production studio open to the public. Designed to serve local and regional musicians, producers, and artists, as well as corporations, agencies, and businesses in need of professional audio and media production, the studio bridges high-end technical capability with a welcoming, creative environment.

A defining feature of Midtopia Studio is its meticulously tuned control and tracking rooms, shaped by legendary engineer and producer David Leonard, best known for his work on Prince's Purple Rain. Leonard's career spans decades of landmark recordings and productions, including collaborations with artists such as Prince, Madonna, Janet Jackson, Paul McCartney, and The Rolling Stones, as well as engineering and mixing for film and television. His deep expertise in acoustics, recording, and sonic precision is woven into the very DNA of the space, ensuring that Midtopia Studio delivers an exceptional listening and tracking experience.

At the heart of the studio sits a flagship API 2448 console—a 24-channel, 30" DAW-bucket desk equipped with classic API 550A 3-band EQs, 560 10-band EQs, and Final Touch Automation. This industry-standard console anchors a professional signal path that includes a Neve 1073DPX, a stereo pair of Empirical Labs Distressors, a Retro Sta-Level, Burl Mothership conversion, Lynx Aurora I/O, and an Avid Pro Tools HDX system running Pro Tools Ultimate on an Apple Mac Studio. Monitoring is handled through Barefoot Audio MM45s with MicroSubs, providing clarity, depth, and translation at the highest level.

Midtopia Studio's microphone and instrument collection reflects its commitment to versatility and sonic excellence. From a Flea 47 Tube and Coles 4038 ribbon pair to Royer R121s, Neumann K184s, Telefunken M81s, and multiple Lauten condensers, the studio is equipped to capture anything from delicate vocals to full bands with character and precision. The live room is complemented by a thoughtfully curated backline, including a Fender Jazz Bass, Silvertone 457 guitar, Vox AC15, vintage Danelectro tube amp, Yamaha U9 upright piano, and a range of modern and classic synthesizers.

Beyond commercial recording, Midtopia Studio is committed to education and workforce development in the creative industries. The studio will offer accessible classes and hands-on training for people of all ages in recording engineering, mixing, mastering, music production, and related disciplines—helping cultivate the next generation of Wichita's audio professionals.

Whether you're a band seeking a world-class tracking experience, a business in need of polished audio or media production, or an aspiring engineer looking to learn from top-tier equipment and professionals, Midtopia Studio is built to create, connect, and elevate.
